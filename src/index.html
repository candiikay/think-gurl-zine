EDITION 01 02

Manifesto

In a world where algorithms shape our reality, we reclaim our agency through critical thinking and collective action.

This zine is our declaration of digital independence—a space where feminist perspectives challenge the status quo and envision new possibilities for technology and society.

We believe that technology should serve humanity, not the other way around. Our mission is to question, critique, and reimagine the digital tools that shape our daily lives.

"The personal is political, and in the digital age, the personal is algorithmic."
EDITION 01 03

Dating Apps

How algorithmic bias shapes our relationships and self-perception.

Dating apps promise connection but often deliver commodification. We explore how these platforms reinforce harmful stereotypes while claiming to democratize romance.

Key Issues:

  • Racial bias in matching algorithms
  • Gender stereotyping in profile systems
  • Data privacy and surveillance
  • Monetization of human connection

Digital Hygiene

Self-care in the age of constant connectivity.

Digital wellness isn't just about screen time—it's about creating boundaries, protecting our mental health, and reclaiming our attention from algorithmic manipulation.

Practical Tips:

  • Set intentional boundaries with technology
  • Curate your feeds mindfully
  • Practice digital minimalism
  • Engage in offline activism

Intimacy

Technology's impact on human connection and vulnerability.

How do we maintain authentic intimacy when our relationships are mediated by screens, algorithms, and corporate platforms?

"True connection requires vulnerability, but vulnerability requires safety—something algorithms cannot provide."

Digital Politics

Feminist organizing in the digital age.

From hashtag activism to decentralized organizing, we explore how technology can amplify feminist voices while navigating the challenges of surveillance and co-optation.
